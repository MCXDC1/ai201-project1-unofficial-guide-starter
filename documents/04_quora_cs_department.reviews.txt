I am a graduate student and PhD candidate in the department of computer science, UConn. I would rate the department as above average in research and funding but If you fall in right hands you may pursue really good research here. Having said that, I should add this very important point that research is something the PhD candidate does. Advisor only guides you. So when it comes to research lot depends on you. There are some professors who does really good research and has a lot of funds. I haven’t worked with all of them but among the profs I know, Dr Sanguthevar Rajasekaran, Dr Jinbo Bi, Dr Ion Mandoiu and Dr. Dong Guk Shin are very well known for their research. All these profs publish papers in the best journals in their areas. Along with them I strongly recommend Dr. Mukul Bansal, Dr Don Sheehy(he works in computational geometry) and Dr Yufeng Wu, if you want to work on areas like Bioinformatics and/or Machine Learning (especially in bio(medical) informatics). Since I work in these areas, I came in contact with only these profs and took their classes. Hence, I am not the right candidate to comment about others. Compared to other uconn depts (like Chemistry, Biology) we have less TA opportunities in CS dept but if you are given admission for PhD you are almost always offered full funding in some form (RA and/or TA).
